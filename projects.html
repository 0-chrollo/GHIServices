Thatch Roof Conversion to Tiles

Thatch tiles (often steel-based, such as Harvey Thatch tiles) are durable, low-maintenance, fire-resistant, and insect-proof alternatives to traditional thatch roofs. They feature a stone-coated finish that mimics the look of thatch while weighing only 0.6 kg/m² — offering long-term savings, reduced fire risk, and enhanced security for homeowners.

  • Fire & Safety: Class A fire rating, reducing lightning risks.
  • Low Maintenance: Weatherproof, pest-proof, and longer-lasting than natural thatch.
  • Lightweight: Approximately 88% lighter than conventional concrete tiles.
  • Aesthetics: Stone-coated, oven-baked finish available in multiple natural colours.
  • Installation: Can be applied over existing thatch roofs or used on new structures.
  • Warranty: Often sold with a 20-year product warranty.

Lightning Conductor Installation, Servicing and Installations

Lightning rod installation involves placing copper or aluminium air terminals at the highest points of a structure — roof ridges, chimneys — connected via conductors to ground rods buried at least 10 feet deep, in line with standards like NFPA 780. Expert installation ensures a safe, low-resistance path for lightning strikes, protecting your property against fire and structural damage.

  • Air Terminals (Lightning Rods): 12-inch or longer rods placed at the highest points, spaced no more than 20 feet apart along ridges.
  • Conductors: Thick copper or aluminium cables connecting the rods to the grounding system.
  • Ground Rods: Copper-clad steel rods, typically 10 feet long, buried deeply to safely dissipate lightning energy.
  • Surge Protectors: Installed to shield electrical systems from surge damage caused by nearby strikes.
